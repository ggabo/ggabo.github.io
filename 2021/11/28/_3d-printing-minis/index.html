3D printing miniatures in PLA

Sun Nov 28 2021
  • 3D
  • 3D Printing

Printing miniatures for wargaming and RPGs using an FDM printer is a daunting task. Since the resolution of those prints is on the lower end of what is necessary to print those small miniatures.

The standard is roughly 25mm height miniature, and with lines of .2 or even .1mm it can get rough. Especially if you're experiencing too much stringing and similar other problems.

I'm testing a .2 mm diameter nozzle to increase the quality of my prints, but I think just that doesn't solve the problem. But at least some key features came up:

  • Having models that don't need support. Usually, with spears and swords pointing up or down, there are no bridges that need support. Not only it's less work to finish, but it also creates an overall sturdier mini;
  • The "heroic scale". Meaning, having heads and arms slightly larger than would be humanly proportionate. Most of the time you'll be looking down at the mini, so some foreshortening is inevitable. This only capitalizes on it, bigger arms and heads mean that the details on them show up better.
  • The larger minis (dragons and stuff) feel less of this problem. It can't be applied to all miniatures, of course, but is something to keep in mind.

Bottom line. 3D printed miniatures in FDM printers are certainly serviceable if you're not interested in having multiple printers (FDM printers tend to be better all-rounders, if you have multiple interests, like me).
